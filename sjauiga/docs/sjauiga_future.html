The future

To us, the Sjauiga project represents two things: First, and foremost, an attempt to rescue previously independent sets of data and make them possible to present to users within the framework for our content management system. In the original plan we stated:

We propose that this is done for the purpose of cleaning the data and for generating a viewer that can operate inside the new web site with the technology available there. However we also propose that we should evaluate METS for a wider deployment in our digitization projects.

That is, we proposed that while we used the package of technology described in this document for solving an acute and imminent, the intention was also to gather experience on possible alternative routes as regards storing and retrieving data from heterogenous sources on digitized objects

Visions

While METS and MODS obviously did the job on a technical level, we still have to think on the whens an whys we this format, or a format like it, would be beneficial.

We start by taking a birds view on the services publish. They range from services providing little more than images that may be browsed (such as the sheet music stored as PDF files in REX) to POMA via services like MANUS, tidskrift.dk and Archive for Danish Literature.

The sheet music in REX contain virtually no textual information what so ever and the only user interface is Adobe Acrobrat. Searching is impossible and the only way to find ones way to a certain piece of content is through browsing. The Gustaf Munch Petersen and Poma on the other hand has several table of contents, focusing on different aspects of the work, there are digital images in high resolution and finally the text is available in transcription. Finally there are various amount of related information that may be of interest for the target audience.

When we move from the stuff containing the images only to the complex services, we find more and more data and more and more data of different type; first elementary metadata identifying the object, then structural metadata describing the relations between content objects inside a work, then to correlations between different instances of the same work (full text and images) and finally correlations and relations between different works.

The power of mets is as an object with high linking power.
