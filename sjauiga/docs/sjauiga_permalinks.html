URI structure and permalinks

Anything on the net without a URL hardly exist.

One goal of the Sjauiga project has been too hide any complications as regards URLs from the users. URLs should be such that they could be reimplemented in the future regardless of technologies. They should be brief enough to print on a folder or business card, and have a potential for being cool.

Before SJAUIGA

      http://base.kb.dk/manus_pub/cv/manus/ManusIntro.xsql?nnoc=manus_pub&p_ManusId=41&p_Lang=main

After SJAUIGA

      http://www.kb.dk/permalink/2006/manus/41/

I.e., the URL length has decreased from 93 to 42 characters

URL Syntax

A Sjauiga URL consists of a prefix:

      http://www.kb.dk/permalink/2006

It is meant that other applications which the www.kb.dk domain should be able to use permalink, and it is understood that the year 2006 refer to the year of publication of the application.

SJAUIGA publishes five applications. Hence:

      http://www.kb.dk/permalink/2006/poma
      http://www.kb.dk/permalink/2006/manus
      http://www.kb.dk/permalink/2006/mus
      http://www.kb.dk/permalink/2006/musman
      http://www.kb.dk/permalink/2006/lum

What follows is work id, user interface language and page number, i.e.,

      http://www.kb.dk/permalink/2006/<application>/<ms id>/<lang>/<page number>/

Example: Page 3a in work 7 in the manus application, encoded in arabic is given as

      http://www.kb.dk/permalink/2006/manus/7/ara/3a/
