1. Introduction

The history of The Royal Library's Digitization programs and Web exhibition stretches some 15-20 years. Much of the material is no longer in shape for structured migration to modern platforms unless very substantial resources are spent on it. This document tries to identify material that are possible to migrate at a lower cost, but still would make a difference. Basically, we want to cherry-pick worthwhile objects.

Below we list resources that are fairly structured to begin with, stuff that are interesting and that were well made by competent people.

The material that are omitted below are either in too poor condition but could just be forgotten. That stuff isn't on the list doesn't imply poor quality, it just means that it is to hard a work to save these web exhibitions. In addition some of this material is now available from the way-back machine.

We list the material under a series of headings or criteria for (or against migration).

We are look forward to input, corrections and proposals.

2.2. Cost of maintenance

Any document delivery is costly. However, it is more so if material are delivered from custom built no longer supported software. The so-called Trigono web apps were made individually for each edition. Any change in user interface or metadata profile were made in Cumulus and then deployed manually on a per edition basis.

After COP we basically only three left.

  • Referenceværker
  • http://dia-prod-mas-01.kb.dk/KBPresse/Site/index.jsp

Of these we feel that Flora Danica and Referenceværker belongs in SIFD

It is problematic to use, run and maintain data in formats or using software or both that are no longer supported by their vendors.

2.5. Fragile connection between metadata and data

A lot of material has been digitized by library staff, published as image or PDF via file systems on servers like img.kb.dk and images.kb.dk and subsequently cataloged in Aleph. This procedure has in retrospect proved to be a poor practice since the connection between metadata and data is too fragile, since the items links used in our CMS and the ones cataloged in Aleph must be maintained manually.

The 42,800 hits from the search

	    -tidsskriftdk site:img.kb.dk filetype:pdf

should give an idea on the magnitude of the problem. (removing the -tidsskriftdk constraint gives 56,900 hits).

There are other materials with fragile connection between metadata and object. Quite a few of the EREZ and Flash objects mentioned above belong to this category. Consider the result of this search in REX. The lion's share of the material is from the manuscript collection's flash/erez objects. There perhaps another 100 specimens from the oriental collections.

2.6. Potential for development

See also Cost of (re-)creation above.

The delivery system which sometimes is called permalink (which doesn't really have a name) contains multipage documents whose structure is described using METS. Migrating them to SIFD would give us an opportunity to develop a structured datamodel for such content. There are around 2800 objects of this kind.

They could also be a starting point for another, richer type of manuscript cataloging based on TEI

  • CODICES LATINI HAUNIENSES
  • FRAGMENTA LATINA HAUNIENSIA
  • Middelalder og renæssance

Since KB doesn't have a system for the handling of finding aids, such material are found elsewhere. If we wanted to create a datamodel for such material, we could start with this archival description.

  • Karen Blixen: Arkivregistratur

The catalog over incunabula at the Royal Library is an interesting representation of a traditional printed catalog.

  • Victor Madsen: Katalog over Det Kongelige Biblioteks inkunabler

ADL is our largest corpus text in TEI. See also XSQL.

  • ADL

3. Hard and too expensive to migrate

There are many digital exhibitions and interesting web sites in the wayback machine, that could perhaps get a new life. The cost to get a sites like old periodica exhibitions or Søren Kierkegaard Manuscript exhibition up and running might not be overwhelming

However, there are few or no opportunities for further development, increased rather than decreased maintenance costs. Furthermore, there are hardly any useful lesson to learn, and the cost of migrating another site will only be marginally lower than the first one since all them are unique.

There will also be a substantial amount of work that cannot be automated.

4. Migration paths

There are different ways we can migrate objects into the new platform.

3.1. Directly to SIFD

All objects that are

  • cataloged in OPAC (Aleph)
  • mainly non-image data

are most readily migrated directly into SIFD. These are (for instance) sheet music in PDF, the objects in the permalink application.

A possible exception could be H.C. Andersen dairies and the Guaman Poma website. These are based mainly on TEI data and could be migrated as a part of a TEI data project together with ADL.

3.2. To SIFD via Cumulus (and consequently possibly COP)

Material that are mainly image data and that delivered via Erez should be migrated via COP.

A special case are those objects stored in Cumulus, but with better metadata in Aleph than in Cumulus itself.
