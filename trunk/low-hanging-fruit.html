1. Introduction

The history of The Royal Library's Digitization programs and Web exhibition stretches some 15 to 20 years. Much of the material is no longer in shape for structured migration to modern platforms unless very substantial resources are spent on it. This document tries to identify material that are possible to migrate at a lower cost, but still would make a difference.

Below we list resources that are fairly structured to begin with, stuff that are interesting and that were well made by competent people. Material that are omitted below are either in too poor condition or just forgotten. That stuff isn't on the list doesn't imply poor quality. We are look forward to input.

2.4. Aging or obsolete formats

EREZ and Flash

EREZ offered an opportunity to publish documents as flash movies. Our EREZ license expired years ago, and the image server is about to be replaced with another product which cannot do page browsing.

There are some important stuff in this format.

  • Klaus Rifbjerg

The material has been produced by OJA and HA and a rough guesstimate yields that there are around 300 of these

DjVu

Some stuff are delivered in DjVu format. The plugin isn't that easy to get hold of and neither is easy to install.

  • Frederik den Femtes Atlas

xsql

  • Illustreret Tidende

Too weak connection between metadata and data

A lot of material were digitized by library staff, published as image or pdf files on servers like img.kb.dk and images.kb.dk and cataloged in Aleph. This procedure has in retrospect proved to be a poor practice since the connection between metadata and data is too fragile.

Quite a few of the EREZ and Flash objects mentioned above belong to this category. See a search in REX

2.5. Potential for development

The delivery system which sometimes is called permalink (which doesn't really have a name) contains multipage documents whose structure is described using METS. Migrating them to SIFD would give us an opportunity to develop a structured datamodel for such content

They could also be a starting point for another, richer type of manuscript cataloging based on TEI

  • CODICES LATINI HAUNIENSES
  • FRAGMENTA LATINA HAUNIENSIA
  • Middelalder og renæssance

Since KB doesn't have a system for the handling of finding aids, such material are found elsewhere. If we wanted to create a datamodel for such material, we could start with this archival description.

  • Karen Blixen: Arkivregistratur

The catalog over incunabula at the Royal Library is an interesting representation of a traditional printed catalog.

  • Victor Madsen: Katalog over Det Kongelige Biblioteks inkunabler
